This repo contains files downloaded from “English-US” directory: <https://github.com/uiuc-sst/g2ps/tree/master/English-US>

This dictionary is practically unusable.

It seems to be generated by some kind of software translating written English into ‘phonemic’ using most common “phonics” rules.

> Note: The pronunciation of about 30% of English words cannot be explained by any “phonics” rules.

This dictionary requires a lot of curating by actual humans.

The file `ISLEdict.txt` is a space-separated file with pronunciation enclosed by the `#` (hash) letters:

```
example(nn,vb) # ˌɛ g . z ˌæ m . p l̩ #
example(nn,vb) # ɪ g . z ˈæ m . p l̩ #
examples(+example+s,nns,vbz) # ɪ g . z ˈæ m . p l̩ z #
exams(+exam+s,nns) # ɪ g . z ˈæ m z #
exanimate() # ɪ g . z ˈæ . n ə . m ɪ t #
exanthem() # ɛ g . z ˈæ n . ɵ ə m #
exanthema() # ˌɛ g . z æ n . ɵ ˈi . m ə #
exanthematous() # ˌɛ g . z æ n . ɵ ˈɛ . m ə . t ə s #
exar() # ˈɛ k . s ɚ #
exarate() # ˈɛ k . s ə . ɹ ˌei t #
```

Pronunciation uses a mix of various Unicode symbols and standard IPA phoneme characters.
